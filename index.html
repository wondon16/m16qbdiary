    2. Film Study Methods

    Ways I studied film this week:

    • Dont just watch highlights ‚Äî watch the entire game flow. This helps you pick up on rhythm, fatigue patterns, substitution strategies, and how teams adjust throughout quarters/halves. Pay attention to the ‚Äúsmall things‚Äù between plays: how players react after mistakes, how the coach signals in plays, or if tempo changes after certain situations (e.g., turnovers, timeouts, foul trouble).
    • Instead of following the ball every time, lock in on your position or the one you‚Äôll be matched against. Notice: Stance and alignment: Are they balanced? Do they tip off run/pass/shot direction? Footwork: Is it explosive or lazy at certain points? Conditioning: Do they gas out after long drives or late in games? By isolating positions, you catch tendencies that the broadcast angle misses.
    • Zoom in on the top threats or your likely matchup. Ask: Do they favor going left or right? Do they always spin a certain way? How do they react to pressure vs. space? What‚Äôs their ‚Äúgo-to move‚Äù when things break down? If you learn a player‚Äôs comfort zone, you can force them away from it.
    • Find their weakness!? Everyone has one ‚Äî it might be physical, technical, or mental. Physical: slow first step, lack of strength, poor conditioning. Technical: sloppy ball handling, weak hand/foot, struggles against zone/press. Mental: frustration after mistakes, slow to adjust, rattled by noise/pressure. Spot these cracks early, because good teams exploit them.
    • Formations, alignments, and spacing usually signal intent. Track how often a certain set leads to a type of play. For example: Trips formation = screen game or quick slant. 5-out = drive-and-kick action. Wing overloaded = iso or pick-and-roll coming. Build a mental chart: ‚ÄúWhen they line up like this, 70% of the time they run X.‚Äù
    • Combine all the above: Pick plays that force their weak hand/foot. Target their tired players late in the game. Design mismatches through switches or screens. The key isn‚Äôt just knowing their weakness, but knowing how to repeatedly stress it until it breaks.

    6A. Pre-Snap Checklist
    • Always get to the line quickly with your hands under center.
      • If the defense is going to shift, they will do it after you get under center.
    • Look for the safeties.
      • Is there anyone in the middle of the field?
    • Look for the best side to read.
      • Number of defenders vs. number of receivers.
    • Look at the defender‚Äôs leverage.
      • Leverage refers to the defender‚Äôs relationship to the receiver, or the area of the field the defender must cover.
    • Know where all the routes are going to be run.
      • Look for possible blitzes.
      • Know where your hot routes are.
    • At this point the QB will have a pretty good idea of where he is going to go with the ball ‚Äî this is extremely important.
    6B. Dropback Reminders
    • Realize you are making the final decision on where to go with the ball on your way back ‚Äî not at the end of your drop.
      • If you haven‚Äôt made a decision by the time you get to the end of your drop, go to the contingency plan right away.
    • With the exception of a quick 3-step drop, take two shuffle-steps up in the pocket max before going to the contingency plan.
    6C. Ten Basic Reads (Top Gun)
    • #1 The Slant Read
      • Key flat defender ‚Äî OLB, strong safety, or corner.
      • Throw away from the flat defender; if he covers the slant, hit the flat or swing route.
      • If he goes flat, hit the slant.
      • The drop matches his leverage: three-big if he is close to the line, three-quick if he is playing off.
    • #2 The Stick Read
      • Key the strong safety or defender who will cover that area.
      • Check pre-snap leverage.
      • Look for the flat, then the stick.
      • Looking to the flat first often opens the stick route.
      • Versus Cover 2, look to the strong-side fade first.
    • #3 The Inside Sideline Read
      • Key the strong safety or defender responsible for that area.
      • Check pre-snap leverage.
      • Look for the swing, then the sideline.
      • Looking at the swing route first often opens the sideline route.
    • #4 The Curl Read
      • Key flat defender.
      • Throw away from the flat defender.
      • If he goes flat, hit the curl.
      • If he drops to the curl, hit the flat or swing route.
    • #5 The Sideline Read
      • Versus Cover 2, the sideline will automatically turn into a fade route.
      • Check corner and safety alignment.
      • Check OLB or strong safety alignment.
      • Key flat defender.
      • If the flat defender can get underneath the route, throw to the stop or seam.
    • #6 The Dig Read
      • Key the underneath coverage.
      • While dropping back, take a look at what the linebackers are doing.
      • If they drop, hit the drag.
      • If they jump or wall the drag, go for the dig.
      • Versus Quarters coverage, the drag usually becomes the safest throw.
    • #7 The Double Square-in Read
      • Key outside linebacker to the strong safety.
      • If the linebacker takes the medium square-in and the strong safety takes the flat, hit the deep square-in.
      • If either defender takes away the deep square-in, throw to the area they vacate.
    • #8 The Deep Out Read
      • Great route versus Cover 2 ‚Äî the flat route will hold the corner.
      • If the corner runs with the wide receiver, hit the flat route immediately.
      • If the corner comes off the wide receiver, continue your drop and hit the deep out.
    • #9 The Deep Reads
      • This is a timing pass.
      • Realize that deep passes are not high percentage completions.
      • Get a good idea where you have the best chance to go with the ball on your pre-snap read.
      • If you are not sure where to go deep with the ball on your third step of a five-step drop, get the ball to an underneath receiver.
      • Four Verticals vs. Cover Three:
        • The inside seams are the first read.
        • Hit the seams as they pass the linebackers at about 15 yards.
        • Look the safety off with a good look at the wide receivers as you drop.
        • Throw away from safety coverage.
    • #10 Man (Single-Coverage) Leverage Reads
      • Where is the blitz coming from?
        • Identify your hot route(s).
      • What are the receiver routes?
        • Think about possible safety help.
      • What are the individual match-ups?
        • Does a particular receiver have an advantage against his defender?
      • Which receiver has leverage on his defender?
        • Which defender is in a bad position to cover the called route?
